Odin's Timeline

  • Odin's birth: Odin is born as the son of Borr and Bestla.
  • Odin's quest for knowledge: Odin seeks wisdom and travels far and wide to learn the secrets of the universe.
  • Odin and the Runes: Odin hangs from Yggdrasil, the World Tree, for nine days and nights to learn the secret of runes.
  • Odin's Sacrifice: Odin sacrifices his eye to gain knowledge from the Well of Mimir.
  • Odin as Allfather: Odin becomes the chief of the Aesir gods and rules over Asgard, the realm of the gods.
  • Odin's ravens: Odin sends his two ravens, Huginn and Muninn, to fly over the world and bring him news each day.
  • Odin and Valhalla: Odin oversees the souls of fallen warriors, whom he gathers in Valhalla to prepare for Ragnarok.
  • Odin and Loki: Odin shares a complex relationship with Loki, who is both a blood brother and a source of chaos.
  • Odin's prophecy: Odin learns from prophecies that Ragnarok, the end of the world, is inevitable.
  • Ragnarok: Odin faces his prophesied death during the battle of Ragnarok.
Posessions - Places - Pets Description
Gungnir Odin's magical spear that never misses its target. Some believe Gungnir is stronger than Thors Stormbreaker and Mjolnir.
Sleipnir Odin's eight-legged horse, the fastest and most agile of all horses. Odin received Sleipnir as a gift from Loki.
Huginn and Muninn Odin's ravens, who fly around the world and bring him information. Huginn represents memory and Munin represents thought.
Valhalla Odin's hall located in Asgard, where he gathers the souls of fallen warriors to prepare for Ragnarok.
Hlidskjalf Considered to be Odins throne, Hlidskjalf was a watchtower located in Asgard.
