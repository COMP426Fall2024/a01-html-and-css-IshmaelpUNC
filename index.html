Norse Mythology! Norse Mythology is a collection of myths and beliefs from the North Germanic peoples, including those who lived in modern-day Norway, Sweden, and Denmark! Much of the mythology tells the tales of various heroes and deities. Central to these myths is the world tree, Yggdrasil, which connects the nine realms, including Asgard, home to the gods, and Midgard, the realm of humans. This website features many of the well-known Norse figures such as the chief god, Odin, is a wise but often mysterious figure, known for his sacrifice of an eye to gain knowledge, while his son Thor, the god of thunder, is celebrated for his strength and protection of humanity. Other important figures include Loki, a cunning trickster whose actions lead to chaos, and Frigg, Odin's wife, known for her foresight. Norse mythology also foretells the apocalyptic event known as Ragnarok, where many gods meet their doom, and the world is reborn in a cycle of destruction and renewal. These myths not only shaped the cultural and spiritual worldview of the Vikings but also continue to influence modern literature, art, and popular culture.

Who is Odin?
Odin is one of the most prominent gods in Norse mythology. He is known as the Allfather and the ruler of Asgard, the realm of the gods.
What are Odin's powers?
Odin is associated with wisdom, healing, death, knowledge, sorcery, poetry, and the runes. He sacrificed one of his eyes in exchange for great wisdom and hung himself from Yggdrasil, the World Tree, to learn the secrets of the runes.
Who are Odin's companions?
Odin is often accompanied by his ravens, Huginn (thought) and Muninn (memory), as well as his wolves, Geri and Freki. He rides an eight-legged horse named Sleipnir.
What is Odin's weapon?
Odin wields a spear called Gungnir, which was crafted by the dwarves. It is said that Gungnir never misses its target when thrown.
What is Odin's role in Ragnarok?
Odin is foretold to play a central role in Ragnarok, the end of the world in Norse mythology. During this cataclysmic event, he will be killed by the wolf Fenrir, but his death will lead to the rebirth of the world.
